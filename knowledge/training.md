# Training (Career Mode)

*Last updated: 2026-07-11*

## Loop

Each career turn: pick a training facility (Speed/Stamina/Power/Guts/Wit), rest, recreation, infirmary, or race. Support cards distribute themselves across facilities each turn; training alongside them raises their **friendship bond**.

- **Friendship (rainbow) training**: once a card's bond is orange+ and it sits on its own specialty facility, training there gives big boosted gains — the core of stat-building. Chaining rainbows is the main skill of career mode.
- **Mood** (Awful → Great) multiplies all gains — keep it up via recreation.
- **Energy** gates training; failures at low energy can cause injuries/bad statuses.
- **Races** give skill points, fans, and stat gains; required races are fixed per character, extra G1s are often worth it for skill points (but cause more fatigue on the turn).

## Support deck

6 cards (1 can be a friend/borrowed card). Deck archetype decides the stat spread — e.g. URA-era standard is 3 Speed / 1 Power / 1 Stamina-or-Guts / 1 Wit, adjusted for the target distance. Cards contribute:

- Training bonuses (the stat gains)
- **Skill hints** (their skill pool is a big part of card value)
- Career events that give stats/energy/skills

## Inheritance (legacy)

Each career starts by picking **2 parent trainees**; their sparks fire at fixed inheritance events:

- **Blue sparks**: stats (+ per star)
- **Pink/red sparks**: aptitudes — the way to raise a distance/surface/style aptitude to S (or patch a bad one, e.g. dirt aptitude)
- **Green sparks**: the parent's unique skill (inherited, weaker version)
- **White sparks**: regular skill hints and race-history bonuses

Spark star count (1–3★) is rolled at career end, influenced by the relevant stat being ≥600 (blue) / aptitude being A (pink). Building good parents ("breeding stock") with 3★ sparks is its own long-term project.

## After career

The finished trainee is frozen: stats, skills, skill points spent. Remaining skill points can still be spent later (this is what the `reference/screenshots/` skill-learn screens are about — post-career or late-career skill purchases).
